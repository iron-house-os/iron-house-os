# Municipal Standards Checklist

Use this checklist during Iron House OS bid intake, drawing review, quantity takeoff, RFQ packaging, estimating, and pre-construction planning. The goal is to catch municipal supplementary-standard requirements before pricing is finalized.

## 1. Project Identification

- [ ] Confirm municipality or authority having jurisdiction.
- [ ] Confirm project address, corridor, road classification, and work limits.
- [ ] Identify whether the project is on municipal, provincial, regional, private, utility, or mixed jurisdiction land.
- [ ] Record tender number, drawing issue date, addenda, and current IFC/IFP/IFT status.
- [ ] Confirm governing standard set, including MMCD edition, municipal supplement, standard drawings, specifications, bylaws, and special provisions.
- [ ] Save source links or file references for all applicable standards in the project folder.

## 2. Documents to Check

- [ ] Tender instructions and contract front end.
- [ ] Civil drawings.
- [ ] Landscape drawings.
- [ ] Electrical or street-lighting drawings.
- [ ] Geotechnical report.
- [ ] Environmental report or ESC plan.
- [ ] Traffic-management requirements.
- [ ] Municipal supplementary specifications.
- [ ] Municipal standard detail drawings.
- [ ] Approved materials list.
- [ ] Testing and inspection requirements.
- [ ] Addenda and Q&A.

## 3. Permits, Approvals, and Notifications

- [ ] Road-use permit required.
- [ ] Highway-use permit required.
- [ ] Traffic-control permit required.
- [ ] Lane-closure approval required.
- [ ] Utility cut permit required.
- [ ] Hydrant use or water connection permit required.
- [ ] Dewatering permit required.
- [ ] Tree permit or arborist approval required.
- [ ] Environmental permit, fisheries window, or streamside protection requirement required.
- [ ] Noise bylaw variance required.
- [ ] Work-hour restrictions identified.
- [ ] Advance resident, business, transit, school, emergency-service, or stakeholder notifications required.

## 4. Utility and Underground Requirements

- [ ] BC 1 Call requirement noted.
- [ ] Utility locates included in schedule.
- [ ] Hydro, gas, telecom, water, sanitary, storm, irrigation, and lighting conflicts checked.
- [ ] Daylighting or hydro-vac required.
- [ ] Utility owner inspection or standby required.
- [ ] Minimum cover requirements confirmed.
- [ ] Pipe bedding and trench backfill requirements confirmed.
- [ ] Imported trench material requirements confirmed.
- [ ] Native backfill acceptance confirmed.
- [ ] Compaction standard confirmed by zone and material.
- [ ] Warning tape, tracer wire, marker posts, and test stations required where applicable.

## 5. Storm, Sanitary, and Water Materials

- [ ] Approved pipe material confirmed.
- [ ] Pipe class, DR, wall type, and bedding class confirmed.
- [ ] Manhole and catch basin standards confirmed.
- [ ] Frame, grate, cover, and adjustment ring standards confirmed.
- [ ] Service connection standards confirmed.
- [ ] Tie-in procedure and shutdown requirements confirmed.
- [ ] Pressure testing, flushing, chlorination, CCTV, mandrel, leakage, or deflection testing required.
- [ ] Supplier RFQs include municipal standard details and approved-material constraints.

## 6. Roadworks and Pavement Restoration

- [ ] Road classification and pavement structure confirmed.
- [ ] Asphalt mix type and lift thickness confirmed.
- [ ] Granular sub-base and base material gradation confirmed.
- [ ] Geotextile or geogrid requirements confirmed.
- [ ] Sawcut, milling, key-in, lap joint, and restoration width requirements confirmed.
- [ ] Curb, gutter, sidewalk, letdown, and driveway restoration standards confirmed.
- [ ] Surface tolerances and proof-roll requirements confirmed.
- [ ] Tack coat, prime coat, and joint sealing requirements confirmed.
- [ ] Pavement-marking restoration confirmed.
- [ ] Testing frequency for asphalt and granular materials confirmed.

## 7. Concrete and Surface Works

- [ ] Concrete strength, exposure class, air entrainment, slump, and cement type confirmed.
- [ ] Reinforcing requirements confirmed.
- [ ] Forming, finishing, curing, and cold/hot-weather requirements confirmed.
- [ ] Sidewalk, curb, letdown, ramp, tactile warning surface, and accessibility standards confirmed.
- [ ] Bollards, sleeves, bike-path details, pads, and miscellaneous concrete items checked.
- [ ] Concrete testing frequency and cylinder requirements confirmed.

## 8. Traffic Control and Public Interface

- [ ] Traffic management plan required.
- [ ] Lane closures, detours, flagging, pedestrian routes, cyclist routes, and transit impacts checked.
- [ ] School, hospital, emergency route, business access, driveway access, and garbage collection constraints checked.
- [ ] Temporary signage, cones, barriers, delineators, plates, ramps, and fencing included.
- [ ] After-hours, weekend, holiday, or night-work constraints checked.
- [ ] TCP supplier or subcontractor RFQ issued where needed.

## 9. Environmental, ESC, and Site Protection

- [ ] ESC plan requirements confirmed.
- [ ] Catch basin protection, silt fence, wattles, check dams, sediment traps, and sweeping included.
- [ ] Dewatering, discharge, filtration, and turbidity requirements confirmed.
- [ ] Spill kit and fuel-storage requirements confirmed.
- [ ] Tree protection fencing, root-zone restrictions, and arborist monitoring confirmed.
- [ ] Dust, mud tracking, noise, vibration, and site housekeeping requirements confirmed.
- [ ] Disposal requirements for contaminated, unsuitable, asphalt, concrete, and excess soil confirmed.

## 10. Testing, Survey, QA, and Closeout

- [ ] Survey layout and as-built requirements confirmed.
- [ ] Compaction testing frequencies confirmed.
- [ ] Concrete testing frequencies confirmed.
- [ ] Asphalt testing frequencies confirmed.
- [ ] Pipe testing, flushing, CCTV, and chlorination requirements confirmed.
- [ ] Inspection hold points confirmed.
- [ ] Material submittals required.
- [ ] Shop drawings required.
- [ ] Record drawings required.
- [ ] O&M manuals, warranties, commissioning, and deficiency-walk requirements confirmed.

## 11. Estimate Impacts to Carry Forward

For every checked standard requirement, confirm whether it affects:

- [ ] Direct labour.
- [ ] Equipment.
- [ ] Material quantity or material type.
- [ ] Subcontract scope.
- [ ] Supplier RFQ wording.
- [ ] Testing allowance.
- [ ] Permit allowance.
- [ ] Temporary works.
- [ ] Traffic control.
- [ ] Environmental controls.
- [ ] Schedule duration.
- [ ] Working-hour restrictions.
- [ ] Risk contingency.
- [ ] Bonding, insurance, or warranty obligations.

## 12. Bid Review Sign-Off

- [ ] Applicable municipal supplement reviewed.
- [ ] Standard drawings reviewed.
- [ ] Approved materials list reviewed.
- [ ] Municipal testing requirements priced.
- [ ] Permit and inspection requirements priced.
- [ ] Traffic-control requirements priced.
- [ ] ESC and environmental requirements priced.
- [ ] Restoration limits and pavement structure confirmed.
- [ ] Drawing conflicts and constructability issues logged.
- [ ] RFQ packages include all relevant drawings, details, and standard references.
- [ ] Estimate assumptions list updated.
- [ ] Exclusions and qualifications drafted.
- [ ] Final bid review completed before submission.
