# Iron House Bid Workflow

## Purpose
This document defines the standard workflow for converting a tender opportunity into a completed bid package.

## Workflow
1. Tender intake.
2. Bid/no-bid review.
3. Download and organize all documents.
4. Review instructions to bidders.
5. Review drawings and specifications.
6. Check addenda.
7. Identify municipal standards.
8. Perform quantity takeoff.
9. Build estimate.
10. Issue RFQs to suppliers and subcontractors.
11. Incorporate supplier pricing.
12. Complete risk review and clarifications.
13. Final estimate review.
14. Assemble submission package.
15. Submit before closing.
16. Archive bid and perform post-bid review.

## Required Deliverables
- Project folder
- Estimate workbook
- RFQ log
- Supplier quote log
- Bid review checklist
- Clarification log
- Submission checklist
- Final bid package

## Gate Reviews
### Gate 1 – Opportunity
- Project fits Iron House capabilities.
- Capacity and bonding confirmed.

### Gate 2 – Technical
- Scope understood.
- Quantities complete.
- Major risks identified.

### Gate 3 – Commercial
- Supplier pricing received or allowances documented.
- Markups reviewed.
- Cash-flow implications considered.

### Gate 4 – Submission
- All forms signed.
- Addenda acknowledged.
- Files validated.
- Submission method confirmed.

## Quality Rules
- Every estimate revision receives a version number.
- Assumptions are documented.
- Exclusions are explicit.
- Supplier quotes are traceable.
- Submission deadlines are never assumed—verify from tender documents.
