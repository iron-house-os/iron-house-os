# Iron House Estimating Standards

## Purpose

This document defines the default estimating standards for Iron House civil construction bids. It is intended to keep every estimate consistent, traceable, and usable for real bid submission, supplier RFQs, project handoff, and field execution.

These standards apply unless a project-specific tender, drawing, specification, municipal supplement, owner requirement, or direct instruction overrides them.

## Estimating Principles

Iron House estimates must be built around the following principles:

- Every number must be traceable to a quantity, unit rate, supplier quote, subcontractor quote, production assumption, or estimating allowance.
- Scope gaps must be logged, not hidden.
- Tender risk must be priced or clearly excluded.
- Municipal standards must be reviewed before final pricing.
- Supplier and subcontractor pricing must be requested early enough to support bid review.
- The estimate must separate self-performed work, subcontracted work, materials, trucking, equipment, rentals, supervision, overhead, markup, and contingency.
- The final bid must be usable for both submission and field turnover.

## Default Execution Model

Unless otherwise directed, Iron House will assume the following execution model.

### Self-Performed Work

Iron House should price the following scopes as self-performed where practical:

- Excavation.
- Trenching.
- Pipe installation.
- Manholes and catch basins.
- Service connections.
- Backfill.
- Compaction.
- Subgrade preparation.
- Granular sub-base and base placement.
- General grading.
- Topsoil placement.
- Cleanup.
- General civil earthworks.
- Basic layout support.
- Material handling.
- Site coordination.

### Rented Equipment Model

Early-stage estimates should assume rented equipment unless ownership is specifically confirmed.

Common rental categories:

- Excavator.
- Skid steer / compact track loader.
- Plate tamper.
- Trench roller.
- Pumps.
- Light towers.
- Generator.
- Small tools.
- Traffic control devices where not supplied by subcontractor.
- Dump trailers or rented trucks where applicable.

### Subcontracted Work

Price the following scopes as subcontracted unless directed otherwise:

- Concrete formwork and finishing.
- Fine grading for asphalt.
- Asphalt paving.
- Pavement markings.
- Street lighting.
- Electrical.
- Specialty traffic systems.
- Hydro-vac.
- Coring.
- Materials testing.
- Survey where professional layout is required.
- Environmental services.
- Landscaping beyond basic topsoil and cleanup.
- Tree protection or arborist work.
- Specialty utility work requiring approved contractors.

## Estimate Structure

Each estimate should be organized into clear sections.

### 1. Project Information

- Project name.
- Owner.
- Engineer / consultant.
- Location.
- Tender number.
- Closing date and time.
- Addenda received.
- Drawing set reviewed.
- Specification set reviewed.
- Site visit requirements.
- Bonding requirements.
- Insurance requirements.
- Substantial completion date.
- Final completion date.

### 2. Scope Summary

- Main work items.
- Self-performed work.
- Subcontracted work.
- Material supply items.
- Allowances.
- Exclusions.
- Qualifications.
- Clarifications.

### 3. Quantity Takeoff

Each takeoff line should include:

- Item number.
- Description.
- Drawing reference.
- Specification reference.
- Unit of measure.
- Quantity.
- Waste factor.
- Notes.
- Source / assumption.

### 4. Direct Costs

Direct costs should include:

- Labour.
- Equipment.
- Materials.
- Trucking.
- Disposal.
- Rentals.
- Subcontractors.
- Testing.
- Traffic control.
- Permits.
- Utility coordination.
- Site protection.
- Environmental controls.

### 5. Indirect Costs

Indirect costs should include:

- Supervision.
- Project management.
- Administration.
- Mobilization.
- Demobilization.
- Small tools.
- Fuel.
- Insurance burden.
- Bonding allowance.
- Safety requirements.
- Temporary works.
- Layout and documentation.

### 6. Markup and Contingency

Each estimate should carry separate lines for:

- Overhead.
- Profit.
- Contingency.
- Startup cost recovery where applicable.
- Truck burden.
- Equipment ownership or rental burden.
- Financing / LOC cost where applicable.

## Quantity Takeoff Standards

### General Rules

- Use drawing dimensions where available.
- If drawings are unclear, use scaled quantities only as provisional.
- Record all assumptions.
- Do not combine unrelated work into one hidden lump sum.
- Include waste factors for materials where appropriate.
- Include trench widening, bedding, surround, backfill, and disposal assumptions.
- Separate sawcut, removal, disposal, replacement, and restoration.

### Earthworks

Track:

- Strip depth.
- Excavation depth.
- Disposal quantity.
- Reuse quantity.
- Imported fill quantity.
- Shrink/swell assumptions.
- Subgrade prep area.
- Granular thickness.
- Compaction requirements.

### Pipework

Track:

- Pipe diameter.
- Pipe material.
- Pipe class.
- Bedding material.
- Surround material.
- Trench depth.
- Trench width.
- Fittings.
- Couplers.
- Tie-ins.
- Thrust restraint.
- Testing requirements.
- Flushing / disinfection where applicable.

### Structures

Track:

- Manholes.
- Catch basins.
- Lawn basins.
- Frames and grates.
- Risers.
- Benching.
- Leads.
- Connections.
- Adjustment to grade.

### Roadworks

Track:

- Sawcut length.
- Asphalt removal.
- Base removal.
- Subgrade prep.
- Granular base.
- Asphalt thickness by lift.
- Tack coat.
- Paving tie-ins.
- Line painting.
- Pavement markings.
- Curb and gutter.
- Sidewalk.
- Driveway restoration.

### Restoration

Track:

- Topsoil.
- Sod.
- Seed.
- Mulch.
- Plantings.
- Irrigation repair.
- Fence repair.
- Private property restoration.
- Cleanup.

## Municipal Standards Review

Before bid finalization, review applicable municipal supplements and standards for:

- Approved products.
- Pipe material requirements.
- Bedding and backfill requirements.
- Pavement restoration limits.
- Testing frequency.
- Compaction testing.
- CCTV requirements.
- Watermain testing and disinfection.
- Traffic management requirements.
- Permit requirements.
- Inspection hold points.
- Tree protection.
- Erosion and sediment control.
- Working hour restrictions.
- Noise bylaws.
- Disposal requirements.

If a municipal standard affects cost or schedule, it must be added to the estimate or logged as a qualification.

## Supplier and Subcontractor Pricing

### RFQ Requirement

Send RFQs for all major supply and subcontracted scopes unless reliable current pricing is already available.

RFQs should include:

- Project name.
- Bid closing date.
- Requested quote deadline.
- Scope description.
- Relevant drawings.
- Specifications where needed.
- Addenda if applicable.
- Delivery location.
- Requested inclusions and exclusions.
- Request to redirect if sent to the wrong contact.

### Quote Review

Each quote must be checked for:

- Scope completeness.
- Addenda acknowledgement.
- Delivery included or excluded.
- Taxes included or excluded.
- Lead times.
- Minimum orders.
- Escalation clauses.
- Exclusions.
- Validity period.
- Bonding or insurance conditions.

## Labour and Production Rates

Production assumptions must be documented by activity.

Each production line should include:

- Crew size.
- Equipment used.
- Daily production quantity.
- Unit of measure.
- Hours per shift.
- Expected site constraints.
- Risk factor.

Common productivity risks:

- Deep trenching.
- Groundwater.
- Rock.
- Utility congestion.
- Traffic constraints.
- Restricted access.
- Night work.
- Weather exposure.
- Hand work.
- Work around existing services.
- Public interface.

## Equipment Costing

Equipment should be priced as:

- Owned equipment burden, or
- Rental cost, or
- Subcontractor-supplied equipment.

Early Iron House estimates should default to rented equipment unless ownership is confirmed.

Equipment cost lines should include:

- Daily / weekly / monthly rental.
- Delivery and pickup.
- Fuel.
- Operator where applicable.
- Attachments.
- Damage waiver.
- Standby time.
- Float / mobilization.

## Trucking and Disposal

Trucking and disposal should be separated from excavation where possible.

Track:

- Material type.
- Quantity.
- Haul distance.
- Truck type.
- Load factor.
- Disposal site.
- Tipping fee.
- Contaminated soil risk.
- Wait time.
- Import backhaul opportunity.

## Traffic Control

Traffic control must be reviewed early. Include:

- Traffic management plan.
- Certified traffic control persons.
- Signage and devices.
- Lane closure requirements.
- Detour requirements.
- Working hour restrictions.
- Municipal permit requirements.
- Pedestrian management.
- Cyclist management.
- Emergency access.

## Testing and Quality Control

Testing allowances should include:

- Compaction testing.
- Concrete testing.
- Asphalt testing.
- Gradation testing.
- Watermain testing.
- Disinfection.
- Pressure testing.
- CCTV inspection.
- Deficiency re-testing allowance.

## Risk and Contingency

Every bid should include a risk review before final pricing.

Common risks:

- Incomplete drawings.
- Missing geotechnical information.
- Utility conflicts.
- Unknown depths.
- Groundwater.
- Contaminated material.
- Tight schedule.
- Liquidated damages.
- Winter work.
- Night work.
- Public access constraints.
- Third-party coordination.
- Long material lead times.
- Permits not in place.
- Aggressive restoration standards.

Contingency should be visible and adjustable. It should not be hidden inside random unit rates without explanation.

## Bid Review Checklist

Before submission, confirm:

- Latest addenda included.
- Tender form complete.
- Bonding included if required.
- Insurance requirements reviewed.
- Taxes handled correctly.
- Unit prices match summary.
- Math checked.
- Supplier quotes reviewed.
- Subcontractor scopes checked.
- Exclusions and qualifications clear.
- Schedule reviewed.
- Completion dates achievable.
- Municipal standards reviewed.
- Risk register reviewed.
- Final price approved.

## Standard Exclusions to Review

Do not automatically exclude these, but review whether they are included, excluded, or qualified:

- GST.
- Bonding.
- Permits.
- Engineering.
- Survey.
- Geotechnical engineering.
- Hazardous materials.
- Contaminated soils.
- Archaeological work.
- Night work.
- Winter conditions.
- Utility relocation by others.
- Hydro, gas, telecom, or third-party utility work.
- Rock excavation.
- Dewatering.
- Landscaping beyond shown limits.
- Irrigation repair.
- Tree protection.
- Offsite improvements.
- Work outside drawing limits.

```text

PROJECTCODE_Project_Name_RFQ_Log_v01.xlsx
PROJECTCODE_Project_Name_Bid_Review_v01.md
PROJECTCODE_Project_Name_Submission_Checklist_v01.md
```

Increase version numbers after major changes.

## Field Turnover Requirement

A completed estimate should support field handoff. Include:

- Final scope summary.
- Key quantities.
- Supplier list.
- Subcontractor list.
- Quotes used.
- Production assumptions.
- Schedule assumptions.
- Risk items.
- Clarifications.
- Exclusions.
- Long-lead materials.
- Required inspections.
- Testing requirements.

## Operating Rule

The estimate is not just a bid price. It is the first version of the project execution plan.
